Why Noise

The internet made everything discoverable. We made belonging intentional again.

Noise is not a feed and your group is not content. There are no recommendations, follower counts, or strangers wandering in from search.

Create a group, share its 12-digit frequency, and let it travel the old-fashioned way: person to person.

01
402177381964

Invite by frequency.

Every group gets a private 12-digit frequency. No searchable directory, no algorithmic discovery, no accidental audience.

02
n 3a:9f:71:0c

Identity without PII.

A Noise ID and password are enough. Add a display name, avatar, and bio without attaching a phone number or email.

03

Encrypted before transit.

Messages and media are encrypted on your device before replaceable relays carry them across the network.

Groups, properly

The room is the product.

Noise starts where other messengers get awkward: large, private groups with their own identity, culture, and control.

  • ✦ Custom icons, backgrounds, and accent colors
  • ✦ Media galleries, replies, and reactions
  • ✦ Rules, moderators, reports, and bans
  • ✦ Encrypted DMs when the group conversation becomes personal

Private by design

Less data.
Less exposure.

Privacy should feel like the absence of friction—not a settings maze you have to understand before saying hello.

01

No phone number

Your contacts do not become a social graph waiting to leak.

02

No email address

Your account begins with a Noise ID, not another piece of personal data.

03

No public search

People join groups because someone chose to share the frequency.

04

Open source

The code is public. The privacy model does not depend on a secret promise.

Strength in numbers

No central switch
to flip off.

Noise uses independently operated relays instead of one central server. More relays mean more paths, more storage, and less dependence on any single company, provider, or machine.
