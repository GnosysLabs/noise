Legal / Privacy

Privacy without
the vague promises.

Noise is designed to work without your phone number, email address, legal name, contact list, or location. This policy explains the information the service still needs to function—and the limits of a network run by more than one operator.

01

Scope and who we are

This Privacy Policy applies to the Noise desktop applications, web application, marketing website, and relays operated by Gnosys Labs (together, the “Gnosys Labs Services”). “Noise,” “we,” “us,” and “our” refer to Gnosys Labs.

Noise is an open relay network. Relays run by other people or organizations are independent services under their operators’ control. Those operators may have their own logging, storage, retention, and legal obligations. This policy does not control what an independent operator does outside the Noise protocol.

02

Information Noise processes

Account and profile information

Noise creates a random Noise ID and cryptographic identity. Your chosen display name, avatar, and bio are optional profile information. Noise does not require a phone number, email address, legal name, address book, birthday, or location to create an account.

Your password is used on your device to derive the credentials that unlock your encrypted account vault. Gnosys Labs does not receive or store your plaintext password and cannot recover a lost Noise ID or password for you.

Messages, group activity, and media

Noise clients create signed group, direct-message, membership, moderation, and profile events. Message bodies and media are encrypted on your device before relay transport. Relays may store encrypted events, encrypted account state, invitation locators, signatures, object identifiers, timestamps, routing information, deletion records, and other protocol data needed to deliver, authenticate, synchronize, moderate, and delete content.

Encrypted media is divided into opaque shards and distributed among a client-selected set of relays. A participating relay stores only the shards assigned to it, either on local storage or an operator-configured S3-compatible service.

Device and browser storage

Desktop apps keep local identity state, message state, preferences, and media caches on your device. The web app stores an encrypted browser vault in IndexedDB and protects it with a non-exportable key generated by your browser. Clearing site data, using private browsing, browser resets, operating-system backups, or device-management tools can affect or copy that local data.

Network and diagnostic information

When your device connects, network providers necessarily process information such as IP addresses, request timing, user-agent information, and traffic volume. Noise can route a storage request through a mask relay so the storage relay sees the mask instead of your direct connection. The mask relay and infrastructure providers may still see network-level information.

The Gnosys Labs website and official relay configuration are not designed for advertising or behavioral tracking. Our hosting, security, and network providers—including Cloudflare—may process request and security data to deliver and protect the service. The marketing site contacts GitHub’s API to locate the current Noise release when a download link is shown.

Communications with us

If you email us or report a problem, we receive the information you choose to send, including your email address and the contents of your message.

03

How we use information

We process information only as reasonably necessary to:

  • create, restore, synchronize, and secure Noise identities;
  • route and make encrypted groups, messages, profiles, and media available;
  • provide moderation, deletion, abuse prevention, and network-integrity features;
  • diagnose failures, protect the service, and improve reliability;
  • respond to support, privacy, and legal requests; and
  • comply with law and enforce the Terms of Service.

We do not sell personal information. We do not use Noise content for targeted advertising, build advertising profiles, or operate a public people or group directory.

04

When information is shared

Information may be processed or disclosed to:

  • Relay operators. The clients you use select relays to carry and store protocol data. Independent relays are controlled by their operators.
  • Infrastructure providers. Hosting, content-delivery, DDoS protection, object-storage, and similar providers process the data needed to provide their services.
  • Other Noise users. People in a shared group or direct-message thread receive the content and profile information you choose to share.
  • Legal and safety recipients. We may preserve or disclose information when reasonably necessary to comply with law, protect rights and safety, investigate abuse, or defend the service.
  • A successor. If the service or organization is reorganized, acquired, or transferred, information may transfer with it subject to applicable law.

Encryption limits what Gnosys Labs and relay operators can read, but it does not stop a recipient from saving, exporting, forwarding, or capturing content after their client decrypts it.

05

Retention and deletion

Local data remains on your device until the app removes it, you log out, leave a group, delete your account, clear browser data, uninstall the app, or remove it through your operating system. Operating-system and device backups may retain copies beyond that point.

Relay data remains available for the time needed to operate the protocol or until it is deleted, expires, or the relay operator removes it. Noise supports signed deletion events and deletion capabilities for encrypted media shards. Deletion can be delayed when a relay, device, or storage provider is offline.

Distributed deletion has real limits.

Noise cannot guarantee erasure from screenshots, exports, backups, modified clients, offline devices, or independently operated relays that ignore or never receive a deletion request.

Support and legal correspondence may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to answer the request, protect the service, and meet legal obligations.

06

Security and early-alpha limits

Noise uses cryptographic identities, signed events, on-device encryption, encrypted account vaults, and distributed encrypted media storage. No system is perfectly secure. Noise is early-alpha software, and its protocol and implementation have not been represented as a completed independent security audit. Do not rely on Noise as a life-safety system or a guarantee of high-risk anonymity.

07

International processing

Noise is available over a global network. Gnosys Labs, infrastructure providers, independent relays, and other users may process information in countries other than your own. Privacy protections and government-access rules vary by location.

08

Your choices and privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection, and to appeal or complain to a privacy regulator. You may also be entitled to know the categories and sources of personal information we process and the recipients to whom it is disclosed.

You can change profile information and use deletion controls in the app. You may also email admin@gnosyslabs.xyz. Because Noise does not require an email address or legal identity, we may need your Noise ID or cryptographic proof from your account to locate data and verify that a request is yours. We cannot decrypt content or recover credentials we do not have.

09

Children

Noise is not directed to children. You must be at least 18 years old to use the Gnosys Labs Services. If you believe a child has provided personal information through a service we operate, contact us so we can investigate.

10

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as Noise changes. We will post the revised policy here and change the “Last updated” date. Material changes may also be announced in the app, on the website, or through the project repository.

11

Contact

Questions, privacy requests, or reports about this policy can be sent to admin@gnosyslabs.xyz.
